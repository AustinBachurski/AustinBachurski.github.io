    • About Me

    I began my self-taught journey into programming January 2021, starting with C++ and expanding into Python, C#, SQL, C, and JavaScript. This followed a successful 18-year career in the mechanical field, where I spent 4 years as a supervisor and 14 years as a mechanic. For the last 1.5 years I have been applying my problem-solving skills in a GIS role. My goal is to transition into a programming career within the next year. I still have a lot to learn, but I'm as driven as they come and I've yet to find something I couldn't become good at if I set my mind to it.

    C++ has been my primary focus as I am drawn to the complexity and control that the language offers. After getting a solid grasp on the fundamentals, I began adding more "tools to the toolbox" starting with Python. My move to a GIS role provided numerous opportunities for automating repatative data entry and mapping tasks, and allowed me to learn and work with SQL via our GIS database. I find myself writing at least one simple script each week to make my workflows more efficient. After running into limitations in speed and portability with Python, I returned my focus to C++. I'll never forget seeing the speed increase for the first time, I thought it didn't work at all because the window closed so fast, then I checked the output file... Pretty sure that is when I got bit by the performance bug, I love making things faster and more efficient.

    After acquiring a C++ Nanodegree from Udacity and getting a few projects under my belt, I took several courses in C#, SQL, C, and JavaScript to broaden my skillset and expose me to additional ways of solving problems. My almost two decades of mechanical diagnosis and repair have given me a "can do" attitude and a unique view to problem solving. Vehicles and equipment now a days are pretty much computers with wheels and electrical diagnosis was my specialty throughout my previous career, maybe some day I'll pursue a career in embedded systems and "return to my roots" in the automotive field. For now, I look forward to learning everything I can about software and systems and honing a new set of skills. It's been a journey already, but I'm just getting started.

    My wife and I had our first child, a baby boy, in December of 2022, so he takes up most of our free time. When I do get a chance to get away I enjoy PC gaming, going to see movies with my wife, and weekend barbeques with friends and family.
